Variance Decomposition — What predicts departure delay?
The full model explains only 1.74% of delay variance. That's not a model failure — it's the finding. The real drivers (inbound plane being late, mechanical issues, gate conflicts) are flight-specific and invisible to any model built on daily weather and airport characteristics.

Model Summary
Carrier identity adds 0.56% to explained variance — more than airport characteristics (0.06%) and about two-thirds of what all weather variables combined contribute (0.83%). Who you fly with matters more than the weather or the airport. Tukey HSD post-hoc tests confirm every season differs significantly from every other except Spring vs Winter. Log-transforming delays improves residual normality but doesn't change the conclusion. Aircraft type adds no predictive power beyond carrier identity.
